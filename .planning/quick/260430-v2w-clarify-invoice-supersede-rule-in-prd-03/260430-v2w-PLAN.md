```
If the client has ordered the plants on credit, an invoice is generated when the order is created on the ERP (not sent to Xero — see Xero Posting Flow below). It is Finance's job to mark the invoice as Paid on the ERP when payment arrives.

**One active invoice per order:** An order can have many invoices over its lifetime, but only one is _active_ at any moment — the most recently generated one. Earlier invoices are kept for audit but marked `Superseded`.

* **Edits before Xero supersede the active invoice.** When the order is edited (line items, quantities, prices) before the final invoice has been posted to Xero, the current active invoice is marked `Superseded` and a new `Unpaid` invoice is generated automatically from the updated line items. The new invoice becomes the active one.

* **Outstanding balance is computed from the active invoice only.** Superseded invoices do not contribute to the order's balance. The "Billing history on order" rollup (see _What It Must Do → General Order Logic_) shows all invoices for transparency, but only the active invoice's totals drive the order-level outstanding amount.

* **Order paid-state is derived, not stored.** There is no `paid` boolean on the Order. An order is considered Paid when its active invoice's `status` is `Paid`. The `invoices` array on Order is the source of truth.

* **Post-Xero corrections go through credit notes.** Once the final invoice has been posted to Xero, it cannot be superseded — the order is locked (`locked_post_xero = true`) and any correction must go through the credit note flow (see PRD-3.3).
```
